What the first chapter of Exodus book of the Bible, describing the creation of humankind, have in common with Object-Oriented programming tenets

2023-05-02 12:54:14

The first chapter of the book of Exodus in the Bible describes the creation of humankind from the dust of the ground. In this story, God creates man in His own image and endows him with a hierarchical structure of dominance and authority. This structure is then used to create the other elements of the world. This same hierarchical structure is also found in object-oriented programming (OOP). In OOP, programmers create hierarchies of objects that can be reused and extended. This hierarchy of objects is used to create more complex systems.

The similarity between the biblical story of man's creation and OOP's hierarchical structure of objects suggests that the bible may have influenced the development of OOP. This is further evidenced by the fact that the concept of reusability is seen in both stories. In the bible, God uses the same hierarchical structure to create the world. In OOP, the same hierarchical structure is used to create more complex systems by reusing and extending objects.

The similarities between the bible and OOP suggest that the concept of object-oriented programming was influenced by the bible's narrative of man's creation. This influence may have helped shape the development of OOP and its hierarchical structure of objects and reusability, leading to the creation of more complex systems.
